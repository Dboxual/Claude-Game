# Zion: Design Bible 0.1

## The promise

“God of War-scale mythology inside a classless, player-shaped sandbox.” The player begins nameless, and becomes legible to the world through deeds: the weapon they practice, the materials they master, the faction they serve, and the relics their warband controls.

## Reward cadence

- Every few seconds: impact, resource pop, damage number, sound-ready particle burst.
- Every 1–3 minutes: crafted item, quest thread, mastery node, useful drop.
- Every 10–20 minutes: build change, new ability, faction rank, new region objective.
- Long arc: warband territory, divine favor, legendary recipes, world-state campaigns.

Rewards must create a new decision—not merely inflate a number.

## Core loop

Explore → gather/hunt → refine → craft a build → fight for relics → gain mastery and favor → unlock harder domains → feed resources and stories back into the player economy.

## Classless combat

The equipped weapon grants the first three active abilities. Off-hand and armor pieces add defensive, mobility, passive, and ultimate choices. Mastery improves effectiveness but should remain horizontal enough that preparation and execution matter.

Initial weapon families: xiphos, spear, labrys, bow, sling, kopis, staff, ritual blades, shield, hammer, chain blades, sun disk, funerary scepter. Each branches into common, divine, and artifact variants.

## Economy and crafting

Raw resources are gathered in dangerous places, refined in cities, and crafted into nearly all usable equipment. Equipment loss and durability will be tuned to maintain demand. Quality, material provenance, crafter mark, enchantment, salvaging, food, alchemy, farming, trade and transport are later milestones.

## Mythological structure

The setting is not one literal mythology. Zion is the shore where broken ages overlap. Pantheons compete over fragments of the First Flame. This permits Greek, Egyptian, Celtic, Mesopotamian, Yoruba, Norse and other story traditions to appear with research, respect, and a coherent original frame.

## Art pillars

- Strong silhouettes and authored color scripts over surface realism.
- Low-to-mid poly forms, chunky geometry, compressed painterly textures.
- Violet-black shadows, bronze highlights, rust red danger, mineral teal magic.
- Dramatic fixed/isometric camera and impossible distant architecture.
- Restrained bloom, haze bands, scanline/dither texture, 4:3-safe UI composition.
- Ornamental UI that remains fast to read: carved frames, compact type, big icons.

## UI principles

- World remains visible behind management panels.
- Bottom-center is combat; bottom-right is meta; top-left is self; top-right is place.
- Every progress system shows the next meaningful unlock.
- Inventory and crafting share item language and support comparison at a glance.

## Near-term milestones

1. Save/load, audio, controller input, larger scrolling world.
2. Equipment swapping and 4 complete weapon lines.
3. NPC forge, bank, market simulation, durability.
4. Dungeon with boss and artifact drop.
5. Multiplayer networking spike, parties and warband roster.
6. Territory campaign and player-driven economy simulation.
