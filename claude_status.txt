TacMove — WIP status (branch wip-m3-content-platform, 2026-07-05, update 4)
============================================================================

Fourth drop on this branch: skill-based melee combat prototype (stamina,
attack phases, parry/riposte/perfect counter, feints, kick, shield, weapon
throwing, duelist training bot). Original design in the spirit of heavy
medieval duel games - no copied values, assets, names, or sounds from any
game. Written by Claude on Windows 11 (MSVC, RTX 4060 Ti).

DONE THIS DROP (built on Windows x64, client + server, /W4 zero warnings)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------
- NEW shared module src/shared/melee.{h,cpp}: the whole combat exchange as
  pure state + pure functions (Actor struct, tick, startAttack, feint,
  upgradeToHeavy, setBlock, resolveStrike). No IO, no pointers, no content
  lookups - deliberately server-serializable so the future authoritative
  server ticks the same code per player. All tuning constants sit at the
  top of melee.h in one block.
- Stamina: 100 max, regen 32/s after 0.9 s, paused while the guard is up
  or staggered. Spent by attacks, feints, kicks, throws, and TAKEN blocked
  hits. Below 30 blocks cost 1.5x; a block that drains to zero = GUARD
  BREAK (1.4 s stagger, stamina refunds to 15 so play resumes).
- Attack phases: windup -> active (the hit window sweeps until it connects
  once) -> recovery, with a combo window in the last 45% of recovery
  (windup x0.65, +2 stamina per chain depth, cap 3). Weapon `weight`
  (new WeaponDef field + cfg key: fists 0.6, karambit 0.75, sword 1.0)
  scales windup/recovery/stamina, so the sword is heavy and fists snap.
- Attacks: SlashLeft/SlashRight (LMB alternates sides; Alt or mouse-X1
  forces the other side), Overhead (wheel up), Stab (wheel down), heavy
  (hold LMB through the windup: +0.22 s windup, 1.6x damage, 1.8x cost),
  Kick (F: 1.3 m, 1.2 s cooldown, opens raised guards - 1.0 s stagger and
  -15 stamina - but only 5 damage on bodies).
- Defense: RMB holds the guard (only protects while Idle - attacking drops
  it). Guard raised within 150 ms of impact = PARRY: attacker staggers
  1.1 s, defender pays 3 stamina, riposte window 0.8 s (next attack winds
  x0.55 and deals x1.25). Late/held blocks are safe but cost 12/20
  (light/heavy) stamina. PERFECT COUNTER: winding up the SAME attack type
  as the incoming strike deflects it for free, staggers the attacker
  1.3 s, and accelerates your attack into a riposte.
- Feint: R or middle mouse cancels the windup for 5 stamina into a 0.18 s
  recovery whose combo window opens fast - cancel INTO another attack;
  stamina is the anti-spam, no free cancels.
- Shield: gear pickup (new `gear = 1` entity flag + EntityDef/WorldEntity
  field so the E-interact ray sees it). Rides along with any melee weapon:
  blocked hits cost x0.6, raised shield forces walk speed (PlayerInput
  walk flag - no new physics), regen pauses while raised, kick opens it.
  Left-arm viewmodel: wooden face + steel boss/rims, raises to cover the
  view, droops when the guard is smashed.
- Throw: Q throws the held melee weapon (never fists, never the glock).
  8 stamina, 15 m/s with a small lob, gravity 20, tumbles end-over-end
  (drawn from the pickup's own visual parts via the new world-space
  orientedBoxes render path). Body hit = 1.1x weapon damage; a raised bot
  guard swats it away; wherever it stops it becomes a normal pickup
  (respawn 0) you can E back into your inventory. Inventory grew
  removeEquipped() for this.
- Duelist Bot (new entity: builtin + server/content/entities/duel_bot.cfg,
  150 HP, respawns 6 s): the sparring partner. Client-side decision loop
  ("no full AI"): near the player it starts a random slow attack (its
  actor runs weight 1.7) every ~1.7-3 s, telegraphed two ways - the blade
  visibly winds to the side the cut comes from (amber as it charges, red
  in the hit window) and a windup_cue sound fires exactly at windup start.
  After attacking it sometimes holds a 2.6 s guard (plain block, never
  parries, never counters - Actor.counters=false). It has its own stamina:
  blocked hits drain it, kicks open it, zero breaks it. Aiming at it reads
  health + stamina + GUARDING/WINDING UP/STAGGERED.
- Feedback: spark particles on every clash (emissive world-space cubes),
  hit-stop on clean hits (sim clock x0.08 for ~50-90 ms; mouse look stays
  realtime), screen shake on heavies/guard breaks (decaying eye wobble),
  red damage flash when struck, stamina bar (green/red/orange by state),
  toasts for PARRY / PERFECT COUNTER / GUARD BROKEN / GUARD KICKED OPEN.
- 7 NEW original sounds (block, parry, shield_block, kick_hit,
  guard_break, throw, windup_cue) added to tools/generate_sounds.cpp -
  same deterministic sine/noise synthesis, zero third-party material -
  regenerated into server/content/sounds/ and all rows added to
  CREDITS.md. Distinct by design: parry rings bright, block knocks dull,
  shield thuds woody, guard break falls apart.
- Viewmodel: melee animation is now driven by the shared actor's phase
  with per-attack keyframes (pull back through windup, sweep to strike,
  settle through recovery) applied to every melee weapon's group - fists
  hook/hammer/jab with the same keys the sword slashes with. Weapon guard
  pose, shield raise/droop, stagger wobble, and a first-person kick boot
  driving up from the bottom of the screen.
- Input: RMB block held state, LMB held (heavy charge), mouse wheel,
  Alt / mouse-X1, F kick, R / MMB feint, Q throw. THE SPAWN MENU MOVED
  FROM Q TO B (Q is throw now) - README and all in-game hints updated.
- Glock untouched gameplay-wise (hitscan path split into its own
  tryAttack guard); dummy_hit stays the bullet-impact sound.
- CMake: melee.cpp added to tac_shared; _CRT_SECURE_NO_WARNINGS defined
  for MSVC (portable sscanf in the config parsers; the _s variants are
  Microsoft-only).

HOW TO TEST THE NEW SYSTEMS (manual, 2 minutes)
-----------------------------------------------
  1. run_windows.bat, Singleplayer -> Start Test World (or --equip sword).
  2. B -> Bots -> DUELIST BOT (spawns ahead), B to close.
  3. Parry: wait for its windup cue sound / amber blade, tap RMB just
     before the strike -> sparks + ring + "PARRY - RIPOSTE NOW"; click
     within 0.8 s for the fast riposte.
  4. Perfect counter: when it winds up an overhead (blade high), answer
     with wheel-up as the strike lands (same type) -> "PERFECT COUNTER".
  5. Kick: wait for it to hold its guard (blade across its front), walk in,
     F -> "GUARD KICKED OPEN", then hit it free.
  6. Guard break (yours): hold RMB and eat hits until stamina empties.
  7. Shield: B -> Pickups -> SHIELD, E to grab, hold RMB (cheaper blocks,
     slower walk); its kick/guard-break rules match the weapon guard.
  8. Throw: with sword/karambit held, Q -> it arcs, tumbles, hits (or is
     swatted by a raised guard), lands as a pickup; E takes it back.

UNFINISHED (known, deliberate)
------------------------------
- No player health: getting hit is feedback (flash/shake/sound), not
  death. Bot damage numbers exist in the exchange but land on nothing.
- Bots do not move, kick, feint, parry, or counter - training partner
  only, full AI is a later milestone.
- Sound-event mapping still client code; combat tuning constants live in
  melee.h, not data files, until the balance settles.
- The server binary still does not simulate combat (melee module is
  shared and ready; wiring lands with server-side simulation).
- Everything from update 3's list that isn't combat: unchanged.

BROKEN / WATCH OUT
------------------
- Sharp edge: WorldEntity/EntityDef grew `gear`; the Interactables ray
  mask now includes gear pickups. Any future pickup kind needs one of
  weaponId/carryable/gear or E will not see it.
- bots_ maps stable entity id -> brain; brains are erased when the bot
  dies so the respawn starts fresh. If you erase entities another way,
  stale brains are harmless (entityById returns null and they are skipped)
  but bots_.clear() on session start is what actually prevents growth.
- The impact pause scales the shared sim clock (accumulator included). If
  a future system must NOT slow during hit-stop, read frameDt, not dt.
